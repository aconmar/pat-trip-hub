Verified from Zambia Department of Immigration directly

Your recollection was right: visa available on arrival

I checked this against the Zambia Department of Immigration's official site (zambiaimmigration.gov.zm) and the Ministry of Home Affairs site. Confirmed:

  • Bottom line
    As a US citizen, you are required to have a visa for Zambia, but you can get it on arrival at Livingstone Airport (LVI) when you land Wed Jul 1. No advance application required.
  • Cost
    $50 USD in cash. Zambia immigration prefers crisp US dollar bills (clean, undamaged, post-2009 series).
  • What you need at the desk
    (1) Your passport (already valid through 2034 ✅), (2) cash $50 USD, (3) full information of your host(s) — physical address where you'll be staying, in your case Mmilo's safari camps. I'll put together a small print-out for you with the addresses closer to departure.
  • Single vs. KAZA Univisa
    Two options. Single-entry tourist visa: $50 — covers Zambia only, valid 90 days. KAZA Univisa: $50 — same price, but ALSO covers Zimbabwe + day trips to Botswana via Kazungula. Same cost, broader coverage.
  • Recommendation
    Take the KAZA Univisa at the same $50. Even though your itinerary doesn't include Zimbabwe, KAZA gives you the option to cross the bridge from Livingstone (Zambia) to Victoria Falls town (Zimbabwe) for a few hours — many travelers find the Zim side of the falls particularly stunning. Mmilo may even offer a half-day Zim Falls excursion.
  • Online option (optional)
    If you'd prefer to skip the on-arrival queue, you can apply for an eVisa in advance at evisa.zambia.gov.zm. Same $50, processed in 3-5 days. Not necessary — Livingstone Airport queues are usually short — but an option if you'd prefer certainty.
