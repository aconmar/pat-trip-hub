🚁 Airlift back to the U.S. — what each says

You explicitly asked: "Would airlift coverage back to the U.S. be covered, or are there restrictions?" Here's what each company's policy says (verified on each company's site tonight):

A. IMG SE + Medjet Annual (recommended for your situation)

  • IMG SE: $500K medical evacuation — but the company chooses the destination (typically nearest adequate facility)
  • Medjet Annual ($295): This is the key piece. Medjet evacuates you to your hospital of choice in the U.S., regardless of medical necessity. No deductibles. No claim forms. Available because you're a US resident under 75.
  • Why this combo: IMG covers the medical bills, Medjet covers getting you home. This is the most common pairing for international travel where you specifically want to come back to the U.S. for treatment.

B. Travel Guard Preferred + Medjet

  • $1M evacuation built in, but again — to "nearest adequate facility," not necessarily the U.S.
  • Adding Medjet gives you the same U.S.-hospital-of-choice benefit.
  • Lower medical max ($100K) — which matters for any complex hospitalization in Europe.

C. Allianz OneTrip Premier + Medjet

  • $1M evacuation. Familiar brand, easy claims app.
  • Medical only $50K — lowest of the four. A serious hospitalization could exceed this in the UK or US fairly quickly.
  • Mixed senior reviews on claims responsiveness.

D. Trawick Safe Travels Voyager + Medjet

  • $1M evacuation, $250K medical — solid coverage for the lowest price.
  • Smaller company, claims process slower per Squaremouth reviews.
  • Good if budget is tight; OK if you're willing to advocate for yourself in a claim.

🎯 My honest recommendation

Given you specifically want airlift to the U.S. and have $14K of pre-paid trip cost: IMG SE + Medjet Annual ($1,800 total). Highest medical cap ($1M) means a serious illness in Europe doesn't drain savings; Medjet guarantees U.S. hospital of choice. If budget is the priority and you accept lower medical max, Trawick + Medjet (~$1,100) is the value pick. Travel Guard (~$1,300) is the middle option with brand stability. Allianz I'd skip — $50K medical is too thin for international travel at 71.
