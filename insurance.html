🎯 My honest recommendation

I'd pick IMG SE + Medjet Annual for these reasons:

  1. $250K medical vs $50K medical is huge for international travel at 71. A serious hospitalization in the UK or Europe can hit $50K in a week — IMG SE has 5× the headroom.
  2. Primary coverage means IMG pays first — you're not waiting on Medicare's slow reimbursement process (which Medicare confirmed to you takes 30+ days for foreign bills).
  3. Pre-existing waiver window is 21 days (vs 15 for TG) — gives more flexibility.
  4. The senior-specific reviews are stronger — U.S. News ranks IMG's family #1 for seniors.

Travel Guard's appeal is real: Brand recognition, simpler website, your friends use it successfully. But the medical max is the deciding factor. If your friends had to use the medical coverage abroad and had a hospitalization, $50K would not be enough.

What both companies share: Medjet Annual is a separate $295 add-on (not Short-Term — your trip is 33 days, Short-Term caps at 30). Medjet evacuates you to your U.S. hospital of choice, regardless of medical necessity. Buy this regardless of which insurance plan you pick.
