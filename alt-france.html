1. Newark → Paris on United

YES — UA flies daily nonstop EWR → CDG.

  • UA 56 EWR 9:35 PM → CDG 10:55 AM next day · 7h 20m · Boeing 767-300ER · Polaris available · all paid miles count toward lifetime million
  • UA 57 return CDG 1:30 PM → EWR 3:55 PM · same aircraft, same Polaris · also counts
  • Cost: Polaris EWR ↔ CDG one-way ~$2,200–$3,800 each direction (similar to LHR pricing)

So EWR ↔ CDG works exactly like EWR ↔ LHR for both Polaris experience and lifetime miles.

2. Mileage comparison

  • EWR ↔ CDG: ~3,630 miles each way · 7,260 round-trip
  • EWR ↔ LHR: ~3,460 miles each way · 6,920 round-trip
  • Difference: France adds ~340 lifetime miles — slightly better for the million-mile goal.

3. What you'd lose if you skipped the Joburg ↔ Newark UA legs

  • EWR ↔ JNB: ~7,970 miles each way · 15,940 round-trip
  • You'd lose those 15,940 lifetime miles — that's the biggest single chunk of the trip.
  • Without Joburg, you'd need to make up ~16K miles elsewhere to still hit the million on this trip. UK-only or France-only routings (without the African legs) probably don't get you there.
Bottom line: Keep the JNB UA round-trip. The math doesn't work without it. Just swap LHR for CDG on the European leg.

4. JNB → Paris (your fourth question)

You're correct — UA does NOT fly JNB → Paris directly.

  • The Star Alliance carriers that fly JNB → CDG/Europe directly are Lufthansa (via Frankfurt) and Air France (not Star Alliance, partner via SkyTeam — no UA miles at all).
  • The relevant Star Alliance carrier would be Lufthansa LH 573 JNB → FRA, then LH/Air France connection FRA → CDG.
  • Star Alliance partner flights do NOT count toward lifetime UA miles — only paid flights on UA/UA Express. So this routing wouldn't help the million-mile goal.
